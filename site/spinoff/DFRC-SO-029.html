Flight Controller Software Protects Lightweight Flexible Aircraft

Case number
DFRC-SO-029
NASA center
DFRC
Category
transportation
First seen
2026-07-20

Lightweight flexible aircraft may be the future of aviation, but a major problem is their susceptibility to flutter_uncontrollable vibrations that can destroy wings. Armstrong Flight Research Center awarded SBIR funding to Minneapolis, Minnesota-based MUSYN Inc. to develop software that helps program flight controllers to suppress flutter. The technology is now available for aircraft manufacturers and other industries that use equipment with automated controls.
