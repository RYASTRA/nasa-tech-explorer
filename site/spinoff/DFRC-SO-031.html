Many of NASA_s aeronautical advances go through testing with unmanned experimental aircraft before they see any commercial flight time. Armstrong Flight Research Center awarded multiple SBIR contracts to Kennesaw, Georgia-based Area-I Inc. for the development of a medium-range, narrow-body, twinjet airliner for aerodynamics investigations. The result is the Prototype-Technology Evaluation Research Aircraft, or PTERA, which is now on offer to research universities and aerospace companies at a fraction of the cost of comparable experimental aircraft.
