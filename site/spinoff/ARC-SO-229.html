Membranes Mimic Kidneys to Filter Water

Case number
ARC-SO-229
NASA center
ARC
Category
public safety
First seen
2026-07-20

Aquaporin A/S, based in Copenhagen, Denmark, approached Ames Research Center in 2007 with a proposal to create filters by infusing membranes with aquaporins, the proteins that let water in and out of living cells. With Ames funding and testing and eight years of development, the company released under-sink reverse-osmosis filters that clean water twice as fast as existing technology, with twice the recovery rate. Aquaporin sees a bigger future market for filtering industrial wastewater using highly efficient forward osmosis.
