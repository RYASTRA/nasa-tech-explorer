Shuttle Topography Data Inform Solar Power Analysis

Case number
JPL-SO-187
NASA center
JPL
Category
environment and resource management
First seen
2026-07-20

In 2000, the Jet Propulsion Laboratory spearheaded the Shuttle Radar Topography Mission, which created a high-detail global elevation map. The data sets were later processed to produce shading models, which are now part of New York City-based Locus Energy LLC's commercial offerings. Locus Energy's solar power prediction packages help companies save millions of dollars in costs by avoiding expensive hardware.
