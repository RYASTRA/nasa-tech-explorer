Simplified Aircraft Modeling Packs Weeks of Analysis into Minutes

NASA center

First seen
2026-07-20

Using software to model how an airplane flies in real-world conditions requires major computing power. Langley Research Center engineer Walter Silva designed software that makes at least one aspect of this modeling much faster, by creating a simplified version of the aircraft structure using a mathematical tool called system identification. The software, called Reduced Order Model, is now available for license, and Huntsville, Alabama-based CFD Research Corporation is using it for current and planned future contracts.
