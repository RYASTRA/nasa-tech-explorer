Models Support Energy-Saving Microwave Technologies

Case number
KSC-SO-126
NASA center
KSC
Category
industrial productivity
First seen
2026-07-20

Kennedy awarded SBIR funding to Troy, New York-based Ceralink Inc., to demonstrate a microwave system that could heat lunar soil to over 2,000 F, temperatures to solidify the surface. Ceralink also examined the feasibility of using computer modeling software to simulate microwave heating on a larger scale. As a result, the team advanced a computer modeling capability that is now incorporated into Ceralink's commercial services.
