Ames Research Center wanted a simple, lightweight device to diagnose astronauts_ illnesses within minutes. Los Angeles-based Holomic, now Cellmic LLC, was hired as a subcontractor to develop a device that used a smartphone to read results from a lateral flow test strip_the same technology as a home pregnancy test_with ultraviolet light. The company had made similar devices using visible light. Cellmic markets its resulting HRDR-300 Fluorescent Immunoassay Reader to companies that develop their own tests.
