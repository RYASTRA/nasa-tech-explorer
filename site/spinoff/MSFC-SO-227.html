Light-Induced Oxidation Cleans Air, Surfaces, Clothes

Case number
MSFC-SO-227
NASA center
MSFC
Category
consumer/home/recreation
First seen
2026-07-20

Under a Marshall Space Flight Center research partnership in the 1990s, scientists at the Wisconsin Center for Space Automation and Robotics discovered photocatalytic oxidation, a process that creates charged hydroxyl radicals that oxidize airborne organic contaminants, turning them into carbon dioxide and water. Dallas-based Aerus Holdings acquired and improved an active form of the technology that emits hydroxyls to purify air and surfaces and has incorporated it into several product lines, even including detergent-free clothes washing.
