Methane Detector Sniffs Out Leaks

First seen

Methane is the main ingredient in the natural gas that powers heating, cooking, and electricity—and a potent greenhouse gas. Methane can also be a sign of bacterial life, which is why NASA installed a methane detector on the Curiosity rover, designing a brand-new tunable laser spectrometer for the task. A different team at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory made it even smaller and faster for methane sniffing on Earth, and Pasadena, California-based SeekOps licensed the technology to detect gas leaks at oil and natural gas wells and more.
