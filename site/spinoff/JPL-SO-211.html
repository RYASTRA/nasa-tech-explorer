Material for Mars Makes Life-Saving Sutures

Case number
JPL-SO-211
NASA center
JPL
Category
health and medicine
First seen
2026-07-20

NASA aims to bring samples back from Mars, but challenges abound: for example, simulated Martian dirt interfered with a clean seal. Techno Planet Inc., building a canister with SBIR funding from the Jet Propulsion Laboratory, turned to Orangeburg, South Carolina-based Zeus Inc. to make a barrier to wipe dirt off the canister edge, which resulted in a new process to get the required properties. Since then, the company has found numerous uses for the extruded material, including as soft, flexible, strong sutures.
