In the 1990s, Jet Propulsion Laboratory engineer Eric Fossum invented what would become NASA_s most ubiquitous spinoff_digital image sensors based on complementary metal oxide semiconductors (CMOS). These were significantly smaller and more efficient than the charge-coupled-device imagers of the day and eventually enabled tiny, battery-friendly cell phone cameras, high-definition video cameras_ such as those offered by San Mateo, California-based GoPro_and social media as we know it. By 2015, the CMOS market reached nearly $10 billion.
