Robotic Joints Support Horses and Humans

Case number
GSFC-SO-165
NASA center
GSFC
Category
health and medicine
First seen
2026-07-20

Cable-compliant joints developed at Goddard Space Flight Center provided the key elements in the NASA Equine Support Technology (N.E.S.T). The device supports a horse's weight with a special harness and controls the pelvis without restricting hip movement. The human version of the technology, released in 2003, is currently assisting U.S. service personnel rehabilitating from spinal cord or brain injuries at Walter Reed Army Medical Center, in Washington, D.C. Enduro Medical Technologies, of South Windsor, Connecticut, expects the N.E.S.T to revolutionize treatment for horses with leg injuries.
